Metabolic is an action agency for societal transformation.

About Us

Metabolic was born out of a desire to realize epic, groundbreaking projects that actively transition the world to a more equitable, sustainable state. We do things that maximize impact. We aim to regenerate natural capital, strengthen social ties among people, and empower organizations to transition to a sustainable future.

Metabolic is both a cleantech development and systems consulting firm. We develop sensing and automation systems for food production and household resource flows, mobile utilities for off-grid activities, and sustainability transition strategies for companies and governments.

Philosophy

We believe in democratizing technology. We design solutions to be affordable and accessible. Most of our work is published open source to encourage replication and fast adoption.

We feel the need to move beyond incremental change. This requires continuously researching, prototyping, and implementing ideas that result in holistic and replicable systems for a sustainable society.

How We Work

Metabolic is powered by a cooperative of multi-disciplinary entrepreneurs who analyze, design, experiment, and build disruptive solutions. We aim to regenerate natural capital, strengthen social ties among people, and empower organizations to transition to a new future.

We work through a data-driven process that takes a systems approach to developing solutions. We integrate existing technologies and stakeholders into our design of systems, which we take from ideation through execution.

Services

Analyze

We translate the complexity of sustainability into clear pathways for decision-making and action for both private and public sector clients. Our main analytical activities include:
  • Strategy consulting for organizations
  • Systems analysis for companies, industries and regions
  • Sustainability assessment and indicator development for system optimization
  • Data visualization for translating and communicating complex information

Design

Metabolic develops sustainable urban metabolisms and circular business models for products and services. Our work is focused primarily on:
  • Feasibility studies and designs for cleantech urban developments
  • Infrastructure design, investment, and transition plans for cities and regions
  • Integrated product design for startups that see us as partners

Experiment

Research and development is core to our work. Understanding local context, testing the efficiency of integrated systems, and experimenting with new materials and technologies. Our work includes:
  • Research and testing cleantech systems
  • Maximizing synergies of energy and nutrients for complex urban farming systems
  • DIY technology and materials workshops

Build

We strive to take projects all the way through to execution. Much of our work starts out as a study or design, but ultimately ends up with creating something tangible. That work includes:
  • Cleantech installations
  • Micro-utility development
  • Products for consumers and businesses
  • Polyculture production systems

Design: Sensor Systems

At our workshop in Amsterdam North, we’re developing small wireless microcontrollers and sensor packages that automate systems while measuring technology performance, environmental quality and user behavior.

Project Portfolio

Cleantech Playground: Sustainable built environments

  • The Cleantech Playground serves as a new blueprint for urban development, where decentralized technologies and full recycling of local resources empowers urban areas to be self-sufficient.
  • The Cleantech Playground was the title of a design and feasibility study of closed cycle, sustainable built environments. Metabolic specifically focused on two neighborhoods in Amsterdam. We published the report in February 2013 with Innovatie Netwerk, part of the Dutch Ministry of Economic Affairs. Click here to read the full report.
  • The concept details how nutrients, energy, and water can be recycled on-site to maximize efficiency of resources. The concept explores the role decentralized technologies play in a resilient urban metabolism.
  • The Cleantech Playground is being applied at the de Ceuvel site in Amsterdam North, creating both a decentralized cleantech utility and a demonstration and testing site for new technologies that can transform how we produce and consume resources and public services in cities.
  • The de Ceuvel development is on a plot of polluted land that will be cleaned using plants (phytoremediation). The buildings on the site will consist of retrofitted houseboats, upgraded to a high level of energy performance.
  • De Ceuvel includes 15 houseboats placed on land and a teahouse that enables creative entrepreneurs, architects, and artists to work in a sustainable environment
  • Throughout the site, solar technologies will convert energy from the sun into heat and electricity. Green roofs and water collection systems are designed to collect, purify, and store rainwater for when it’s needed. Sanitation systems will extract energy, nutrients, and water from the waste produced for on-site food production. A network of sensors provide information on performance and user behavior. The site will serve as a “Cleantech Playground” where new technologies can be tested, building a pathway for their broader adoption.
  • The de Ceuvel neighborhood has involved a variety of public and private partners to explore new opportunities for applied research for a sustainable built environment.
  • Metabolic works with partner Circle Economy to analyze and map opportunities and strategies for improving circularity at a regional level.
  • With the Circle Economy, we have developed the Circle Scan - a process and methodology that identifies key impact areas and helps organisations quickly identify and realize a circular project.
  • We work with a variety of organisations to identify their ambitions and capacity to realize a circular project. During our workshops with Circle Economy members, we use a systems thinking approach to identify and prioritize high impact opportunities.
  • Circle Economy member Xindao is an international producer of promotional products. We have helped them map out and prioritize a number of circular opportunities to pursue.
  • The built environment is responsible for a significant amount of impact globally. With the Circle Economy, we have helped re-developer OVG to investigate strategies to incorporate circular thinking into real-estate development.
  • Metabolic is using existing sensors combined with our own micro-controller design to measure technology performance, environmental quality, and user behavior.
  • We use sensors to automate urban farming systems, measure resource use in the built environment, and monitor air and water quality for cities.
  • Metabolic develops new uses for sensors and automation at our workshop, Metabolic Works, located in Amsterdam North.
  • We’re working to incorporate our sensing and automation systems in new applications that can make us more knowledgeable about our environments while we make our environments more responsive.
  • The Overhoeks Tower is an iconic building in Amsterdam that used to be the headquarters of Royal Dutch Shell. It was bought with plans to transform it into a series of clubs, apartments, and restaurants. During this transition, it was opened up to unique start-ups and creative businesses who could make good use of the space in the interim.
  • Metabolic transformed the first floor of the Overhoeks Tower into an open-plan co-working space and testing ground in the Summer of 2013.
  • The Overhoeks Tower served as Metabolic’s headquarters until we moved our offices to the New Energy Docks in November, 2013.

  • A video of Metabolic in June shows us retrofitting the Overhoeks tower and preparing greywater treatment systems during the same weekend.
  • Symbioculture presents a radical departure from business as usual in agricultural production: rather than focusing on the idealized production of a single crop (as a monoculture), it instead proposes the production of many crops, fungi, livestock, and insects in a complex polyculture. It is designed to approximate some aspects of a natural ecosystem with self supporting plant and animal interactions, and the creation of ecological “niches” within the system the reduce need for mechanical and chemical interventions.
  • Metabolic is currently applying the concept of Symbioculture in the Netherlands with a Dutch growers in partnership with Innovatie Netwerk and Rabobank.
  • ‘Polydome Applied’ illustrates the practical application of symbiotic agricultural (symbioculture) systems that maximize food output, eliminate environmental impact, connect biophysical and social flows, and create meaningful jobs. Read the full report online
  • Metabolic is also applying symbioculture to urban agriculture. The Symbiotic Agro-Makery (SAM) is low-cost system that supports the production of mushrooms, fish, herbs, and greens, while including electricity-powered logistics and a community gathering place.
  • SAM cascades energy, nutrients, and gasses throughout the system. It is designed to maximize the synergies that result from integrating cycles of material and energy.
  • Thailab works with communities in Chiang Mai. Thailand to improve access to basic services like water, sanitation, energy, food, and shelter. We combine local knowledge of available material, needs, and preferences with expert advice and open-source designs to build Do-It-Yourself (DIY) technologies that are useful, affordable, and replicable.
  • Current challenges call for new forms of sustainable development. Wastewater treatment is painfully inadequate in Thailand, exacerbating problems that come with flooding and water pollution. The urban poor need to be must be given the right empowerment to improve their quality of life.
  • Thailab is the first project by Metabolic Foundation, a non-profit arm of Metabolic. It is the result of 10 months of on-the-ground research by 2 researchers from Utrecht University.
  • Metabolic is developing an automated window farming system that is productive, beautiful, customizable, and easy to use.
  • The technical system combines aeroponics and sensors to make farming in the home simple but engaging.
  • Our technical team has been exploring ways to automate and monitor the window farm at low cost.
  • In the Summer of 2013, we began building prototypes to improve the product’s functionality and aesthetics.
